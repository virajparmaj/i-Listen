## Design Context

### Users
iListen is for creators, musicians, archivists, and operators converting their own rights-cleared video or music sources into portable audio libraries. They need a clear batch workflow, trustworthy legal reminders, metadata control, and export-ready reports without visual clutter.

### Brand Personality
Retro, precise, and utility-minded. The interface should feel like an iPod Classic and early iTunes utility: familiar chrome, warm off-white panels, graphite terminals, LCD blue accents, and a few restrained vintage copper notes.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the Claude-approved screenshot split: native Apple-style UI/body text for readability; DM Serif Display for headings; Limelight for deco brand/eyebrow moments; Special Elite for technical/status accents; VT323 for LCD playback, timing, bitrate, and progress numerals; SF Mono only inside the terminal logs panel. Keep the theme silver, off-white, graphite, LCD blue, and lightly vintage rather than modern SaaS gradients.

### Design Principles
- Preserve dense, readable workflows before adding decorative type.
- Use retro fonts only where they add character or encode a technical state.
- Keep body text, buttons, controls, and long-form inputs calm with native UI typography.
- Tokenize colors and typography so design passes do not drift the theme.
- Avoid broad redesigns when the existing iPod Classic system already carries the product.
